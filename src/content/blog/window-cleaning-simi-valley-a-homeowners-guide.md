## Why Simi Valley Homes Need Regular Window Cleaning

Simi Valley sits in a narrow east-west valley surrounded by the Santa Susana Mountains to the north and the Simi Hills to the south. This geography creates specific conditions that make window cleaning more important than in many other parts of Southern California.

### Santa Ana Winds and Inland Valley Dust

Simi Valley experiences some of the strongest Santa Ana wind events in Southern California, with gusts regularly exceeding 40 mph during fall and winter months. According to the [National Weather Service Oxnard office](https://www.weather.gov/lox/), the Simi Valley and Conejo Valley corridors are designated High Wind Warning zones during Santa Ana conditions. These winds carry fine dust from inland deserts and deposit it heavily on exterior windows over just a few days.

### Wildfire Smoke and Ash

The [California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection](https://www.fire.ca.gov/) designates much of Simi Valley as a Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zone. The 2018 Woolsey Fire and the 2003 Simi Fire both left widespread ash and smoke residue on homes throughout the valley. Ash bonds to glass surfaces and, without professional-grade cleaning, can permanently etch the surface. The [U.S. Environmental Protection Agency](https://www.epa.gov/smoke-ready-toolbox-wildfires) recommends professional cleaning for exterior surfaces after significant smoke events.

### Hard Water from Valley Wells and Municipal Supply

Simi Valley is served by Ventura County Waterworks District No. 8, which sources from both the State Water Project and local groundwater. Water hardness in the valley averages 15 to 20 grains per gallon — classified as "very hard" by the [Water Quality Association](https://www.wqa.org/). Sprinkler overspray and hose rinsing leave mineral deposits that begin etching glass within 30 to 60 days if not removed.

## How Often Should Simi Valley Homes Clean Their Windows?

Frequency depends on the home's position in the valley and its exposure to wind, irrigation, and traffic.

- **Every 3 months**: Hillside homes in Wood Ranch, Big Sky, or homes near the 118 freeway where wind and traffic dust accumulate rapidly
- **Every 4 months**: Standard residential properties in Central Simi, Sinaloa, or Madera neighborhoods
- **Every 6 months**: Homes in protected pocket neighborhoods with minimal sprinkler exposure and low traffic

The [National Association of Home Builders](https://www.nahb.org/) recommends cleaning windows at least twice per year to maintain glass integrity. In a wildfire-prone, wind-exposed valley like Simi, Aloha Window Bros typically recommends the higher end of this frequency.
