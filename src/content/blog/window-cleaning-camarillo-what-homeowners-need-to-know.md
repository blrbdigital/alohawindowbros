## Why Camarillo Homes Need Regular Window Cleaning

Camarillo sits between the Santa Monica Mountains and the Pacific coastline, creating an environment where windows collect debris from several directions at once.

### Salt Air from the Coast

Camarillo lies roughly seven miles inland from the Oxnard coastline, close enough that ocean breezes carry salt particles inland on a daily basis. According to the [National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration](https://www.noaa.gov/), coastal marine aerosols can travel 5 to 10 miles inland under typical Southern California wind patterns. Salt residue bonds to glass and, left untreated, can etch the surface and cause premature seal damage on double-pane windows.

### Agricultural Dust from the Oxnard Plain

The farmland surrounding Camarillo, including the Oxnard Plain to the west, generates significant airborne dust, especially during harvest seasons. The [California Air Resources Board](https://ww2.arb.ca.gov/) reports that agricultural operations are among the top contributors to PM10 particulate matter in Ventura County. This fine dust settles on exterior window glass and creates a dull, grey film within weeks.

### Hard Water from Local Irrigation

Camarillo is served by both the City of Camarillo Public Works and the [Calleguas Municipal Water District](https://www.calleguas.com/), which delivers water with hardness levels averaging 15 to 20 grains per gallon — classified as "very hard" by the [Water Quality Association](https://www.wqa.org/). Sprinkler overspray and exterior hose rinsing leave mineral deposits that become permanently etched into glass within 30 to 60 days.

## How Often Should Camarillo Homes Clean Their Windows?

Recommended frequency depends on the property's exposure.

- **Every 3 months**: Homes in the Mission Oaks area and properties within two miles of the 101 freeway, where traffic and agricultural dust accumulate quickly
- **Every 4 months**: Standard residential properties in neighborhoods like Village at the Park, Spanish Hills, or Woodside Greens
- **Every 6 months**: Homes with minimal sprinkler exposure and no direct agricultural or coastal wind exposure

The [National Association of Home Builders](https://www.nahb.org/) recommends cleaning windows at least twice per year to maintain glass integrity and prevent permanent mineral etching. In a coastal-adjacent city like Camarillo, Aloha Window Bros recommends the higher end of this frequency.
