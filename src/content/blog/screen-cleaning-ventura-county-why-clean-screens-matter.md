## How Dirty Screens Affect Your Home

Window screens are designed to let fresh air in while keeping insects out. But over time, screens accumulate layers of dust, pollen, cobwebs, and environmental debris that fundamentally change how they perform.

According to the [American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE)](https://www.ashrae.org/), obstructed window screens can reduce natural ventilation by 30–50%, forcing homeowners to rely more heavily on HVAC systems. In Ventura County, where mild weather makes open-window living possible 8–10 months per year, that's a significant impact on both comfort and energy costs.

> "A standard fiberglass window screen with moderate dust and pollen buildup blocks 30–50% of potential airflow. In Southern California's mild climate, homeowners who keep screens clean can reduce air conditioning usage by 10–15% during spring and fall months."

> "Homes with professionally cleaned screens report noticeably better indoor air quality during peak pollen season. When screens are clogged with pollen, opening a window defeats the purpose — you're pulling concentrated allergens directly into your living space."

## Signs Your Screens Need Cleaning

Not sure if your screens are overdue? Check for these indicators:

- **Visible dust layer** — run a finger across the mesh; if it comes away gray or brown, the screen needs cleaning
- **Reduced airflow** — windows feel stuffy when open, or you notice less breeze than expected
- **Allergy symptoms increase when windows are open** — dirty screens push allergens indoors
- **Cobwebs in corners** — spiders nest in neglected screens, and their webs trap additional debris
- **Water doesn't pass through easily** — hold the screen under running water; if it pools rather than flowing through, the mesh is clogged
- **Screens look hazy or discolored** — a clean screen should be nearly invisible when you look through it

## Screen Maintenance Between Professional Cleanings

Aloha Window Bros recommends these simple steps to extend the life of your screens between professional cleanings:

- **Quick vacuum** — use a brush attachment on low suction to remove surface dust monthly
- **Rinse after Santa Ana events** — a gentle hose rinse prevents dust from bonding to the mesh
- **Check for damage** — small tears get bigger over time; flag them early for repair
- **Keep landscaping trimmed** — branches and leaves pressing against screens cause staining and damage

### Can dirty screens make allergies worse?

Yes. The [Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America](https://www.aafa.org/) notes that clogged screens concentrate pollen and particulates, pushing them into your home when windows are open. Clean screens act as a filter rather than an allergen source. This is especially important in Ventura County where oak and grass pollen seasons overlap for much of the year.

### Should I clean screens separately or with window washing?

Aloha Window Bros recommends bundling screen cleaning with window washing for the best value and most complete results. Screens are already removed during window cleaning, so adding professional screen washing is efficient and costs less per screen than standalone service.

### How do I know if my screens need replacing instead of cleaning?

During every screen cleaning, Aloha Window Bros inspects each screen for tears, bent frames, loose mesh, and broken hardware. If a screen is damaged beyond what cleaning can fix, the crew will let you know so you can arrange a replacement. Small tears often get worse over time, so catching them early saves money.

### Do you clean sliding door screens too?

Yes. Aloha Window Bros cleans all types of screens including standard window screens, sliding door screens, security screens, and retractable screens. Each type requires slightly different handling, which is why professional service is recommended over DIY for specialty screens.
