## Why Santa Barbara Windows Get Dirty Faster

Coastal California homes face unique challenges that inland properties do not. According to the [National Association of Home Builders](https://www.nahb.org/), salt air corrosion is one of the top maintenance concerns for properties within 5 miles of the ocean. Santa Barbara sits directly on the coast, exposing every window to these conditions daily.

The primary culprits include:

- **Salt spray and marine fog** — leaves a white, hazy residue that bonds to glass
- **Hard water mineral deposits** — Santa Barbara's water supply contains calcium and magnesium that create permanent etching if left untreated
- **Pollen from coastal vegetation** — oak, eucalyptus, and palm trees produce heavy seasonal pollen
- **Construction dust** — ongoing development in the Santa Barbara area adds fine particulate to the air

> "Coastal windows exposed to salt air degrade up to 40% faster than inland windows when left uncleaned, according to glass industry research. Regular professional cleaning is the most cost-effective way to extend window lifespan in oceanfront and near-ocean homes."
